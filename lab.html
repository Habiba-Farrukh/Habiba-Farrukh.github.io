The Security and Privacy of Emerging Computing Technology (SPECT) Lab is led by Prof. Habiba Farrukh in the Department of Computer Science at the University of California, Irvine. Our research investigates security and privacy threats in emerging computing platforms—including smartphones, wearables, AR/VR headsets, and IoT devices—and designs secure systems that are technically robust and aligned with users' needs.

Research

Our research spans the following areas:

AR/VR & Extended Reality Security

Investigating security and privacy threats in augmented and virtual reality platforms, including spatial data leakage, UI attacks in WebXR, and eye gaze privacy in XR environments.

Mobile & Wearable Security

Analyzing permission models, side-channel attacks, and authentication mechanisms on smartphones and wearable devices such as smartwatches.

IoT & CPS Security

Studying secure device pairing, physical event verification, and side-channel vulnerabilities in IoT and cyber-physical systems.

Usable Security & Privacy

Understanding users' security and privacy concerns, attitudes, and behaviors across emerging platforms including conversational AI and online ecosystems.
